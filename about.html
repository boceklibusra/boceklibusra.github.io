About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in geotechnical earthquake engineering with a background in civil engineering and architecture. My research focuses on ground-motion modeling, DYFI intensity data, and spatial correlation of earthquake shaking.

I have experience in teaching and laboratory instruction in soil mechanics and foundation engineering, where I support students in understanding experimental concepts and data interpretation.

My work combines research, teaching, and engineering practice, with an emphasis on clear technical communication and interdisciplinary collaboration.

  • Ground-motion modeling and seismic hazard analysis
  • DYFI intensity data integration
  • Teaching and laboratory instruction
  • Interdisciplinary engineering research
