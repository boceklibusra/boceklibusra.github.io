About Me

I’m a Ph.D. student in geotechnical earthquake engineering with a background in civil engineering and architecture. My research focuses on ground-motion modeling, DYFI intensity data, and spatial correlation of earthquake shaking. I am also passionate about teaching, technical communication, and interdisciplinary engineering research.

  • My research and technical writing center on improving seismic hazard understanding by integrating community-reported shaking data with instrumental records, especially for regions with sparse station coverage.
  • I work on ground-motion models, path attenuation, and nonstationary spatial correlation, with particular interest in California earthquake datasets and DYFI-informed PGA estimation.
  • I have experience as a teaching assistant and lab instructor in soil mechanics and foundation engineering, where I support students through experiments, grading, and technical explanations.
  • My academic goal is to continue contributing to research, teaching, and engineering practice through work that connects earthquake engineering, data analysis, and practical problem-solving.
  • In addition to academic work, I value clear communication, collaboration, and mentorship, and I enjoy building bridges between technical research and real-world application.

I hold degrees in civil engineering and architecture, and I am currently pursuing my Ph.D. at Old Dominion University. You can view my background, experience, and recent work through the links on this page or by opening my CV.
