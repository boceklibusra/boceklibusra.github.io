I am a Ph.D. student in geotechnical earthquake engineering at Old Dominion University. My work focuses on ground-motion modeling, DYFI intensity data, and spatial correlation of earthquake shaking for improved seismic hazard understanding.

Current Focus

I study how community-reported earthquake intensity data can complement sparse instrumental records and strengthen ground-motion modeling in California and beyond.

Research.

Ground-Motion Modeling

I investigate attenuation behavior, variability, and predictive relationships between observed shaking and engineering intensity measures such as PGA.

DYFI & Instrumental Data

I compare community-reported shaking intensity with instrumental recordings to evaluate consistency and improve models where station coverage is limited.

Spatial Correlation

I study nonstationary spatial correlation decay rates in California to better understand regional differences in earthquake ground motion.

Teaching.

Laboratory Instruction

Student Support

Technical Communication
